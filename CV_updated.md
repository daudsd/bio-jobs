## MSc Thesis — Development of Dry Powdered Cod Liver Oil

Investigated the production of a dry powdered cod liver oil using enzymatic tissue dissociation, freeze drying, and spray drying. Screened processing conditions to optimize encapsulation of EPA and DHA using food-grade excipients (acacia gum, fish gelatin, bovine gelatin). Assessed fatty acid composition, oxidative stability, sensory properties, and microstructure.

**Key findings:**
- Acacia gum selected as preferred excipient (17.03% yield, wider food-grade acceptance)
- Atomization at 400 L/h gave optimal spray drying performance
- Quantified EPA+DHA losses across drying methods and storage conditions (40°C, 75% RH)
- Identified biological variation in fatty acid content (12.5–19.8% EPA+DHA) across fresh cod livers
